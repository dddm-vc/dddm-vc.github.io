DDDM-VC: Decoupled Denoising Diffusion Models with Disentangled Representation and Prior Mixup for Verified Robust Voice Conversion

Abstract

Recently, diffusion-based generative models have shown their powerful generative performance. However, since there are many attributes in the data distribution and some limitations of sharing model parameters in the entire generation process, it is still challenging to control specific styles for each attribute. To address the aforementioned problem, this paper presents decoupled denoising diffusion models (DDDMs) with disentangled representations, which can control the style for each attribute in generative models. We apply DDDMs to voice conversion (VC) tasks where there still remain challenging problems in disentangling and controlling each attribute of speech (e.g., linguistic information, intonation, and timbre). First, we utilize self-supervised representation to disentangle speech. Then, DDDMs are utilized to resynthesize speech from disentangled representations to denoise with respect to each attribute. Moreover, for robust voice style transfer, we also propose a prior mixup, which utilizes the converted representation by the mixed style as a prior distribution for the diffusion models. The experimental results demonstrate that our method outperforms publicly available VC models. Furthermore, we show that our method has robust generative performance regardless of model size (Specifically 5.87x smaller than state-of-the art VC system.).
